Automation · Expansion
aLex Linen Machine Expansion
South Brooklyn Health · Tower Building
Following the successful deployment of 10 aLex linen machines in the new Ruth Bader Ginsburg building, an opportunity emerged to extend that success to the existing Tower building nursing units — replacing traditional linen carts with smart automation.

I coordinated across three distinct stakeholder groups: IPA (the machine manufacturer), hospital facilities teams including IT and engineering, and senior hospital executives on the client side. Together we scoped machine quantities, determined optimal placement across nursing units, and finalized sizing requirements for each location.
12 machines delivered & installed across Tower building nursing units — driving a 40% reduction in weekly linen costs ($20K → $12K) while improving inventory tracking and strengthening infection control compliance. Timeline: ~12–18 months

TDSS Linen Software Implementation
NYC Health + Hospitals · 16 Sites
The existing linen tracking system was outdated — extracting meaningful data for KPI reporting was difficult, and frontline accountability gaps were impacting operations. A new solution was needed that could scale across a large, complex hospital network.

I participated in the test pilot program, working alongside frontline linen staff to validate the system's ability to capture delivery data in real time and surface that information to management. My involvement helped shape the product before its network-wide rollout, including coordinating with NYC Health + Hospitals IT teams to get the software whitelisted across all site networks.
Successfully rolled out to all 16 NYC Health + Hospitals sites — giving management real-time inventory visibility and holding frontline staff accountable for every delivery and pickup logged. Timeline: ~2 years (pilot to full rollout)

Program Launch · Compliance
Lab Coat Rental Program
South Brooklyn Health · Hospital-Wide
SBH physicians had no standardized in-house lab coats — a compliance gap that posed infection control risks and left doctors purchasing and laundering their own. I identified an opportunity to solve it through an existing vendor relationship.

I sourced and pitched a lab coat rental program to hospital executives, secured client approval, and managed the full implementation: coordinating fittings for 350+ users over 2–3 months, tracking sizing data in Excel, collaborating with Unitex on production, and working with the client to design embroidered SBH-branded coats with personalized credentials for senior staff. I also determined locker placement throughout the facility for seamless clean coat distribution and soiled coat pickup.
350 users outfitted with 3 personalized lab coats each — improving infection control compliance, elevating SBH brand visibility, and saving physicians time and out-of-pocket costs. Timeline: ~18 months
